Manual·Field Doctrine

Four ideas every cadet carries.

  1. §01

    Speak in codes, not opinions.

    The radio is a shared instrument. Say only what dispatch, supervisors and every unit in the field can act on. Ambiguity is friction, and friction costs seconds.

  2. §02

    Cite the statute, then the human.

    Every stop, arrest, and citation begins with a section — but ends with a person. Precision protects both sides of the badge; kindness is not a weakness of law.

  3. §03

    The console is a partner.

    The MDT does not replace judgment; it accelerates it. Train the reflexes until the interface disappears and only decisions remain.

  4. §04

    Leave the shift better than you found it.

    Reports filed, kit accounted, radios docked, cadets briefed. The last discipline of a good tour is a clean handoff.
